Why?

The academic publishing market dominated by a handful of large publishing companies. At the same time, writers feel a strong “publish or perish” pressure, readers suffer the high prices for access, and small Journals struggle to break into the market. This need to change now.

How?

The key to publishers’ dominance is the monopoly of the peer-review and this is where we are different. With μr², peer-reviews happen before Journals receive the Article.

Just imagine a huge number of peer-reviewers online who would filter the Articles before they go to the publishers. The Writers could offer they articles to multiple Journals at the same time, the Peer-reviewers get fair rewards for their job, and the new and/or smaller Journals don’t need to make the unequal fight for peer-reviewer with the big ones. This system could be beneficial for everybody except for the monopolies.

When?

Soon! The journey has already begun. The first step was to develop an editor where it’s possible to write and collaborate on Articles, which is already in place. The second step was to match unpublished Articles, with potential peer-reviewers using AI, and the final step is to build a community around the μr² project. This is where you come in.

Do you need an easy to use, versatile and comprehensive text editor for academic writing with equations and diagrams? The μr² editor does exactly this by extending Markdown with LaTeX equation support.
Reasons to try our editor:

  • It’s fast; the simple formatting saves a significant amount of time over other word processors
  • It’s easy to learn; Markdown syntax is so simple that one can barely call it “syntax”
  • The user interface is minimal, helping to focus on what you are writing
  • Fully supports LaTeX math input
  • Easily export your document to PDF, Microsoft Word, ePuB, LaTeX and HTML
  • Alternatively, publish your work directly to Wordpress.com, Medium.com or on our page

The editor uses plain text formatting syntax aimed at making writing simple. The philosophy behind it is that a document should be readable without tags everywhere but still allow text modifiers such as lists, bold text and italics. For example, to emphasize words, you simply enclose them in * (asterisks). So, *emphasize word* would look like emphasize word in the final document. If you are new to Markdown or need help with the syntax, do not worry. You can use the toolbar controls within the editor or open up a Markdown cheatsheet from the menu.

Most academic articles are the result of shared effort by multiple Writers. To help Writers work together, the μr² supports collaborative real-time editing. Any registered user is welcome to work on your document and you will be able to edit it simultaneously.

Open-Access Article search engine

Tens of thousands of Open-Access scientific Articles are published every year in Math, AI, Biology and Geographical areas. This raises two questions:

  • How can one easily keep up to date?
  • And how can one find articles of high quality in a sea of publications?

Our search engine and Open-Access Article database aim to answer these questions.

First, we make a strict selection of over 250 000 Articles from 430 Journals[1][2] in these areas. This helps reduce “noise” and means that users only see good Articles.

Second, our Artificial Intelligence is running in the background. It finds related Articles based on the contents of the abstract. This leads to a much more accurate search experience than any keyword-based approach – try it here. Moreover, if an Article is of interest, it can be subscribed to, and when the AI finds similar newly published articles, you will receive an email notification. With this, you can keep up to date with minimal effort!
