Why?

The academic publishing market dominated by a handful of large publishing companies. At the same time, writers can feel a strong “publish or persist” pressure, readers suffer the high prices of access, and small Journals struggle to break into the market.

How?

The key to publishers dominance is the monopoly of the peer-review and this is where we are different. With μr², peer-reviews happen before Journals receive the Article.
Just imagine a huge resource of peer-reviewers online who filter the Articles before going to the publishers. The Writers could offer they article to multiple Journals same time, the Peer-reviewers should get fair rewards for their job, and the new and/or smaller Journals shouldn’t need to make the unequal fight for peer-reviewer with the bigs. This system could be beneficial for everybody except for the monopolies.

When?

Soon! The journey has already begun. The first step was to develop an editor where it’s possible to write and collaborate on Articles. The second step was to match unpublished Articles with potential peer-reviewers using AI, and the final step is to build a community around the μr² project. This is where you come in.

Do you need an easy to use, a versatile and comprehensive text editor for academic writing with equations and diagrams? The μr² editor does exactly this by extending Markdown with LaTeX equation support.

Reasons to try our editor:

  • It’s fast; the simple formatting saves a significant amount of time over other word processors
  • It’s easy to learn; Markdown syntax is so simple one can barely call it “syntax”
  • The user interface is minimal, helping to focus on what you are writing
  • Fully supports LaTeX math input
  • Easily export your document to PDF, Microsoft Word, ePuB, LaTeX and HTML
  • Alternatively, publish your work directly to Wordpress.com, Medium.com or in our page

The editor uses plain text formatting syntax aimed at making writing simple. The philosophy behind it; a document should be readable without tags everywhere but still be able to add text modifiers such as lists, bold text and italics quickly. Here’s a quick example; to emphasize words, you simply enclose them in * (asterisks). So, *emphasize word* would look like emphasize word in the final document. If you are new to Markdown or need help with the syntax do not worry. You can use the toolbar controls within the editor or open up a Markdown cheatsheet from the menu.

Most academic articles are the result of shared effort by multiple Writers. To help Writers work together the μr² supports collaborative real-time editing. Any registered user can be invited to work on your document and you will be able to edit it simultaneously.

Open-Access Article search engine

Tens of thousands of Open-Access scientific Articles are published every year and this raises two questions:

  • How can one easily keep up to date?
  • And how can one find good quality Articles in a sea of publications?

Our search engine and Open-Acces Article database aims to answer these questions.

First, we make a strict selection of over 250 000 Articles from 430 Journals[1] in Math, AI, Biology and Geographical areas. This helps reduce “noise” and means that users only see good quality Articles.

Second, is our Artificial Intelligence running in the background. It finds related Articles based on the contents of the abstract. This leads to a much more accurate search experience than any keyword-based approach - try it here. Moreover, if an Article is of interest, it can be subscribed to, and when a new article is published that the AI thinks is similar, you will receive an email notification. With this, you can keep up to date with minimal effort!

  1. All of these Journals are in the Web of Science: Science Citation Index Expanded or Emerging Sources Citation Index. However, if you think a Journal is missing, please do not hesitate to contact us on our Github project page. ↩︎
